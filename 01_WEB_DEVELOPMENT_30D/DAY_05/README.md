## 🧠 Discoveries: AI for Video
* **Image-to-Video (I2V) > Text-to-Video (T2V):** Giving the AI an existing high-quality image (like the UI assets I generated yesterday) and asking it to animate it yields much more controllable and consistent results than generating video purely from text.
* **Prompting for Camera Movement:** You have to explicitly command the "camera" in your prompts. Using words like "slow pan," "drone shot," or "cinematic tilt" changes the entire feel of the video.
* **Generation Cost & Time:** AI video takes significantly longer to render than images and burns through credits quickly. It's crucial to refine the prompt perfectly before hitting generate.

## 📖 Terminology Dictionary
*The vocabulary needed to direct AI video tools like a cinematographer.*

### Video Generation Terms
* **I2V / T2V / V2V:** Image-to-Video, Text-to-Video, and Video-to-Video. The different prompting methods.
* **Frame Rate (FPS):** Frames per second. Cinematic video is usually 24fps, while smooth digital video is 60fps.
* **B-Roll:** Supplemental or alternative footage intercut with the main shot. Great for website backgrounds.
* **Hallucination / Morphing:** When the AI video loses consistency and objects start melting or changing shape unnaturally over time.

### Camera Movement Terms
* **Pan:** Moving the camera horizontally left or right.
* **Tilt:** Moving the camera vertically up or down.
* **Tracking Shot:** The camera physically moves through space, following a subject.
* **Depth of Field (DoF):** The distance between the nearest and furthest objects that are in focus. A "shallow" DoF means a blurry background.
